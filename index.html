About

Hello, and thank you for visiting! I am Ali Karimi, a visiting researcher at the University of Stavanger in the Department of Energy and Petroleum Engineering, under the supervision of Professor Mohsen Assadi. Additionally, I am a Ph.D. student in industrial engineering at the University of Naples Federico II, Italy, and a proud member of the Apper Lab research group at the University of Naples Federico II, where I work under the guidance of Professor Alfredo Gimelli.



My current research focuses on the following areas:

  • Hybrid Energy Systems: Hybrid energy systems combine multiple sources of energy, such as renewable and conventional, to create efficient and sustainable energy solutions. I study the integration and optimization of these systems for enhanced performance and reduced environmental impact.
  • Digital Twins: Digital twins are virtual representations of physical systems or processes. I explore the use of digital twins to model and simulate energy systems, enabling better monitoring, control, and decision-making.
  • Optimization: Optimization techniques play a crucial role in improving the efficiency and cost-effectiveness of energy systems. I apply optimization methods to enhance the performance of various energy-related processes.
  • Artificial Intelligence in the Energy Sector: Artificial intelligence (AI) holds great potential in transforming the energy sector. I leverage AI methods for data-driven modeling and monitoring of energy systems, aiming to enhance performance and reduce emissions.

These research areas drive my passion for creating innovative solutions that contribute to a more sustainable and efficient energy landscape.
